Offer Accepted

Timescale: 4-9 Weeks
Overview

Congratulations, You have accepted an offer on your property. Welcome to the timeline where you will receive updates and helpful tips on your property sale. Ensure you complete your responsibilities to achieve the fastest completion time.

Your Conveyancer's Responsibilities

1. Draw up draft contract pack and send to you.

2. Send approved draft contract pack.

3. Respond to buyer’s enquiries on your behalf.

Your Responsibilities

1. Ensure that you have completed the following:

    TA6 Property Information form.

    TA7 Leasehold information form (if applicable).

    TA10 Fixtures and Fittings form.

    TA13 Completion Information form.

2. Send any building work documents to your conveyancer.

3. Review draft contract pack.

4. Request final redemption statement from your mortgage provider.

5. Respond promptly to any buyer enquiries.

6. Negotiate contract with buyer via your conveyancer.

Sensei Wisdom

Be as truthful as possible on all forms you fill out.  Anything other than the truth leaves you open to financial penalties. If you don’t know, simply state “I don’t know”.

Offer Accepted

Timescale: 1 Week
Overview

Congratulations, you have had your offer accepted.  Welcome to the timeline where you will receive updates and helpful tips on your property purchase. Ensure you complete your responsibilities to achieve the fastest completion time.

Your Conveyancer's Responsibilities

Provide a quote with a breakdown of their services and the associated costs.

Your Responsibilities
1. Check that Next Chapter Homes have taken the property off the market.
2. Appoint a conveyancer to begin the legal process.
3. Inform Next Chapter Homes of your chosen conveyancer.
4. If you will be taking out a mortgage, complete your mortgage application and submit. This is not the same as an Agreement in Principle.

Optional: Appoint a surveyor for an independent survey.

Find out more about surveys here

Sensei Wisdom

Don’t just choose the cheapest or closest conveyancer, research their hidden costs and reviews. Location is almost irrelevant these days and a company that can efficiently communicate with you is much more valuable than location. Don’t forget to ask any potential Conveyancer about planned holidays.

Mortgage Valuation

Timescale: 1-2 Weeks
Overview

You should now be awaiting a mortgage valuation from your provider. Mortgage valuations assess the property to determine whether the agreed sale price is within a reasonable range of the market value. Should the valuation not meet the sale price, you can request a reduction in sale price, increase your deposit or cancel the purchase.

Your Conveyancer's Responsibilities

1. Send document which outlines the terms of their services and costs to you.

2. Carry out ID checks and request payment for expected upfront fees.

3. Request Contract Pack from the seller’s conveyancer.

Your Responsibilities

1. Review your conveyancer’s terms of services and sign any documents agreeing to the terms and conditions.

2. Provide any requested ID.

3. Pay any outstanding fees to allow searches to take place.

Sensei Wisdom

Save time by requesting all read only documents to be sent to you electronically rather than post.

You are now at:

Buyer Enquiries

Timescale: 1-2 Weeks
Overview

After performing various checks in regards to the property, you will receive a report of your conveyancer’s findings, along with a copy of the leasehold information pack if applicable. The report will present the findings from the searches requested, the contents of the contract pack, their disputes and recommendations.

Your Conveyancer's Responsibilities

Await instruction from you

Your Responsibilities

Review the contents of the report thoroughly and raise enquiries on any discrepancies, remember you have seen the property and your conveyancer hasn’t.

Especially check:

    1. Property Information – Check that land boundaries and match up with your understanding.

    2. Fixtures & Fittings – Make sure this matches with what was negotiated with the seller.

    3. Leasehold Information Pack – Clearly understand the terms and conditions of the leasehold.

    4. Building Regulation Approval – Ensure all applicable work carried out has been granted approval. Find out what is covered here. 

    5. Properties with Extensions – Cross reference with your local authority search to ensure planning permission in addition to building regulation approval.

Sensei Wisdom

Arrange an additional viewing of the property to review any items unsure of in the report and also check if progress has been made on many minor issues the seller agreed to fix before the sale.

You are now at:

Enquiries & Searches

Timescale: 2-4 Weeks
Overview

Your now have a confirmed mortgage offer. By now your conveyancer should have received a contract pack off of the seller’s conveyancer and requested searches. The contract pack contains a draft contract, and property information forms amongst other information. The searches requested look into the local environment. If the property is leasehold, a leasehold information pack is also required to be sent by the freeholder. Your conveyancer will act in your best interests to raise enquiries on any discrepancies found within these documents.
Your Conveyancer's Responsibilities

1. Raise enquiries with regards to contract pack and leasehold information pack.

2. Request searches.

3. Obtain Mortgage offer from your mortgage provider.

4. Produce a report of their findings and send to you for review.

Your Responsibilities

1. Get in contact with your conveyancer to ensure that progress is being made. Remember, they work for you.

2. Start researching into removal companies and buildings insurance (if required)  in preparation.
Sensei Wisdom

Request your report to be sent electronically to save time. Also, if your property is leasehold, ensure the leasehold management pack has been released by the management company - this can cause delays.

Sign Contract

Timescale: 1-4+ Weeks
Overview

After any issues are resolved your conveyancer will send you a final contract in preparation for exchange and completion.

Your Conveyancer's Responsibilities

1. Send you contract and transfer deed 

    Transfer deed not always required

2. Agree exchange and completion dates with all relevant parties

3. Send completion information form and transfer deed to seller’s conveyancer for completion

4. Carry out pre-completion searches

Your Responsibilities

1. Review, sign and send back contract and transfer deed to your conveyancer.

2. Agree exchange and completion dates with your conveyancer.

3. Take out buildings insurance for the proposed date of exchange.

Not always applicable to leasehold flats

4. Transfer deposit to conveyancer.

5. Finalise date for removal company if required.

Sensei Wisdom

It is common to have exchange and completion on the same day. However If you plan to do this and have a Help To Buy ISA, ensure that your conveyancer requests the bonus in time (5 days before completion).

Exchange Contracts

Timescale: 1-4+ Weeks
Overview

Your conveyancer has now exchanged your contract with the sellers contract. Both you and the seller are now contractually bound to completing the property transaction. Failure to complete may lead to financial penalties for the responsible party.

Your Conveyancer's Responsibilities

1. Exchange contracts with seller’s conveyancer.

2. Transfer deposit to seller’s conveyancer.

3. Apply for mortgage funds to be transferred to their account.

4. Send Stamp Duty Land Tax form to you.

Your Responsibilities

1. Sign mortgage deed and send to your conveyancer.

2. Sign Stamp Duty Land Tax form and send back to your conveyancer.

3. Plan tactics for moving day.

Sensei Wisdom

Ask around local shops for free cardboard boxes which will come in handy for moving day.

Consider ordering broadband for your new home. Set-up can take up to 6 weeks.

Completion!

Timescale: 1 Day
Overview

Congratulations, you have completed your purchase with Next Chapter Homes and Housensei. Happy moving day and enjoy your new home!

Your Conveyancer's Responsibilities

1. Transfer purchase funds to seller’s conveyancer.

2. Complete land registry application.

3. Upload land registry title to Help to Buy ISA Portal if applicable.

4. Pay stamp duty tax on your behalf.

5. Receive title deeds and transfer deed from seller’s conveyancer.

6. Send title deeds to your mortgage lender.

7. Prepare completion statement and send to you.

Your Responsibilities

1. Pick up your keys.

2. Take meter readings for utility switching.

3. Move in!

4. Pay any outstanding fees from the completion statement.

Sensei Wisdom

Order a takeaway for the first night. After a day of moving in you will deserve it! Also look out for your legal documents which will come in the post a few days after completion - keep them safe!

Overview

Your conveyancer has now exchanged your contract with the buyer’s contract. Both you and the buyer are now contractually bound to completing the property transaction. Failure to complete may lead to financial penalties for the responsible party.

Your Conveyancer's Responsibilities

4. Obtain figures owed to mortgage provider and estate agent.

5. Send completion statement with breakdown of the sales funds.

Your Responsibilities

1. Complete TR1 Transfer form and return to your conveyancer.

2. Prepare for moving out.

3. Check all agreed fixtures and fittings are present.

Sensei Wisdom

If possible, move out before the planned completion date to prevent last minute stresses.

Always be in the loop, no matter your location


During a property transaction, it is frustrating experience to call your estate agent and conveyancer multiple times a day to get answers about your latest status.

Offer Accepted

Timescale: 1 Week

The seller accepts the buyer's offer on a property. Surveyor is sent on the buyer's behalf to review the condition of the property in depth.
Mortgage Valuation

Timescale: 1-2 Weeks

The buyer's proposed mortgage provider carries out a valuation of the property in order to determine whether the agreed sale price is within a reasonable range of the market value.
Enquiries & Searches

Timescale: 2-4 Weeks

The buyer's mortgage offer is confirmed and a contract pack is produced by the seller's conveyancer. The buyer's conveyancer makes enquiries regarding the contents of the contract pack and requests searches into the local environment. A report is produced and sent to the buyer to review.
Buyer Enquiries

Timescale: 1-2 Weeks

The buyer reviews the contents of the report produced by their conveyancer and makes their own enquries and requests to their conveyancer as a result.x
Sign Contract

Timescale: 1-4+ Weeks

Both the buyer and seller agree on the final terms which are written into two individual contracts for each party to sign. Dates for exchange and completion are agreed.
Exchange Contracts

Timescale: 1-4+ Weeks

The two parties exchange contracts via their respective conveyancers. A deposit is transferred to the seller's conveyancer and each party is obligated to proceed with the sale. Failure to complete may lead to financial penalties for the responsible party.
Completion

Timescale: 1 Day

The buyer's conveyancer sends the agreed funds to the seller's conveyancer in return for the keys and deeds for the property.
